# Clowder & Crest — Characters

Working character sheet for the main guild cast. These are not just breed stereotypes; they are the story roles each cat fills in the guild’s emotional and thematic structure.

## The Founding Wildcat

**Role in the story:** protagonist, founder, and emotional center of the guild.

The Wildcat begins as the stray in the storm: soaked, hungry, and alone under the lean-to behind the grain market. More than any other character, the Wildcat embodies the game’s rags-to-riches premise. They are not important because of pedigree or status. They become important because they are the one who stayed, worked, and built something where nothing existed before.

**Core traits**
- fierce
- independent
- territorial
- stubborn
- protective

**Surface want**
- survive
- earn enough fish to keep going
- build a guild that can endure

**Deeper need**
- learn that leadership is not the same thing as carrying everything alone
- accept that a home is something shared, not merely defended

**Story function**
- gives the game its underdog credibility
- anchors the “home” theme
- turns the guild from shelter into chosen family

## Russian Blue

**Role in the story:** quiet heart, observer, and the guild’s gentlest conscience.

The Russian Blue is soft-spoken, perceptive, and emotionally precise. Where the Wildcat leads through force of will, the Russian Blue leads by seeing what others are feeling before they say it aloud. This cat is one of the clearest ways the story communicates that tenderness is not weakness.

**Core traits**
- gentle
- loyal
- reserved
- sensitive
- observant

**Surface want**
- safety
- trust
- a place where quietness is not mistaken for fragility

**Deeper need**
- believe that their voice matters even when spoken softly

**Story function**
- softens the Wildcat without domesticating them
- gives the guild an interior emotional vocabulary
- reinforces the game’s quieter, melancholic tone

## Tuxedo

**Role in the story:** organizer, quartermaster, and advocate for order.

The Tuxedo is the cat who wants systems, categories, lists, and proper procedure. In another story this character could become cold or comic; here they work best as someone whose discipline is genuinely useful to a fragile institution. The Tuxedo helps turn the guild from a survival arrangement into something legible and sustainable.

**Core traits**
- sharp
- formal
- focused
- exacting
- civic-minded

**Surface want**
- competence
- order
- professionalism

**Deeper need**
- accept that not everything meaningful can be measured or controlled

**Story function**
- represents institution-building rather than mere survival
- gives friction to more impulsive personalities
- makes the guild feel increasingly real as it grows

## Maine Coon

**Role in the story:** protector, stabilizer, and proof that gentleness can be strong.

The Maine Coon is physically imposing but emotionally warm. This cat should feel like the guild’s safest pair of paws: someone who can carry burdens, keep watch, and make a room feel less frightening simply by being in it. The Maine Coon turns protection from an abstract ideal into a lived experience.

**Core traits**
- patient
- social
- dependable
- physically powerful
- kind

**Surface want**
- protect the others
- be useful
- keep the guild secure

**Deeper need**
- understand that being needed is not the same thing as being loved

**Story function**
- gives the guild emotional and physical ballast
- reinforces the “found family” theme
- makes the guildhall feel like a defended home rather than just a workplace

## Siamese

**Role in the story:** visionary, spiritual interpreter, and dramatic truth-teller.

The Siamese brings voice, flair, and intuition. This cat is the easiest bridge between the grounded guild-management loop and the game’s saint-haunted Catholic mythlore. They should feel half mystic, half performer: someone whose instincts can sound theatrical right up until they prove correct.

**Core traits**
- vocal
- dramatic
- intelligent
- intuitive
- spiritually sensitive

**Surface want**
- to be heard
- to make meaning out of events
- to interpret what kind of guild this is becoming

**Deeper need**
- distinguish true insight from the desire to be seen as special

**Story function**
- gives language to the sacred side of the setting
- raises the emotional temperature of dialogue scenes
- keeps the guild’s moral and spiritual identity in view

## Bengal

**Role in the story:** late recruit, destabilizer, and proof that loyalty can be chosen.

The Bengal enters later than the core guild, which makes this cat structurally important. The Bengal should not feel like “just another recruit.” They are the cat who arrives from outside the guild’s original identity and therefore tests whether the guild is strong enough to absorb contradiction. Fast, restless, and brilliant, the Bengal injects productive volatility.

**Core traits**
- high-energy
- restless
- brilliant
- improvisational
- dangerous in the best and worst ways

**Surface want**
- motion
- challenge
- a place big enough for their talent

**Deeper need**
- believe that commitment is not captivity

**Story function**
- personifies the rival-guild aftermath
- tests whether the guild can turn victory into belonging
- gives later bond arcs a more volatile rhythm

## Key Relationship Axes

These are the most important character tensions in the current cast:

- **Wildcat / Russian Blue**: force and tenderness, leadership and trust
- **Wildcat / Tuxedo**: instinct and structure
- **Wildcat / Maine Coon**: scrappy founder and steadfast protector
- **Wildcat / Siamese**: pragmatism and meaning
- **Wildcat / Bengal**: earned loyalty versus restless ambition

## What makes this cast work

The cast works best when each cat is treated as:
- a specific personality, not just a breed fantasy
- a different answer to the question “what should this guild become?”
- a different relationship to home, work, and belonging

The game’s strongest character writing happens when a conversation is not merely about a task, but about how that task reveals each cat’s private belief about safety, usefulness, love, or faith.
