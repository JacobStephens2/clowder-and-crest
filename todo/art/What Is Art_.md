### Comprehensive Analysis

All three models converge on a core insight: art is the intentional use of human creativity to produce something that communicates meaning, emotion, or experience beyond mere function. This convergence across independently reasoning models suggests high confidence in this as a working definition—broad enough to encompass cave paintings and video games, specific enough to exclude purely utilitarian objects. Each model also agrees that no airtight philosophical definition exists, and that the question "what is art?" has been actively contested since Plato.[^1_1][^1_2][^1_3][^1_4]

The models present the same major philosophical traditions—mimesis, expressivism, formalism, and institutionalism—but emphasize them differently. Gemini 3.1 Pro Thinking provides the most historically structured account, walking through these paradigms chronologically from classical antiquity through conceptual art. Claude Opus 4.6 Thinking gives each tradition roughly equal weight and adds the Wittgensteinian anti-definition stance, which holds that "art" is a family-resemblance concept with no single essence—a position neither of the other models raises. GPT-5.4 Thinking bypasses the academic taxonomy in favor of a concise personal synthesis: art is "a human attempt to give form to feeling, thought, or perception so that it can be shared."[^1_5][^1_3][^1_4]

The most interesting tension concerns **whether skill is essential**. GPT-5.4 Thinking explicitly states a painting can be "technically weak but still be art if it genuinely expresses or provokes something," privileging intention and effect over craft. Gemini 3.1 Pro Thinking, by contrast, opens with skill and imagination as central to the definition. Claude Opus 4.6 Thinking splits the difference, listing craft as one of several intersecting factors. This disagreement mirrors a real philosophical divide—the institutional and conceptual traditions de-emphasize skill (Duchamp's readymades require none), while formalist and functional traditions foreground it.[^1_7][^1_3][^1_1][^1_5]

GPT-5.4 Thinking's craft/design/art trichotomy is a uniquely practical contribution: it gives you a quick mental model for categorizing creative work, even though the boundaries blur in practice. Claude Opus 4.6 Thinking's mention of AI-generated art as the "latest frontier" is timely and connects this ancient question to 2026's ongoing debates about authorship and originality. Gemini 3.1 Pro Thinking personalizes the response by linking the concept to interests like social dance, Tchaikovsky, video game design, and literary fiction—making the abstract concrete.[^1_6]

If you want a single sentence to carry away: art is what happens when someone deliberately shapes material, sound, movement, or language to share an experience that ordinary communication cannot fully capture. The ongoing inability to define it more precisely is itself part of what makes it art.[^1_2][^1_8]
